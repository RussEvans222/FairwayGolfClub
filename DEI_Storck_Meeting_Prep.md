## Anticipated Questions & Prepared Answers

**"How do you build in a historic building without a lengthy preservation review?"**
Everything is modular and freestanding — simulator enclosures and the sloped floor system are self-contained wood-and-metal frame pods that sit inside the room like furniture, with nothing anchored into or altering the historic envelope. Restroom plumbing uses code-compliant above-floor macerating systems (Saniflo) instead of cutting into the concrete slab, except where wet-wall access is already available. This is a deliberate strategy to avoid triggering full historic-preservation review, not something we're hoping slips through — happy to walk through the specifics with your preservation/permitting staff.
