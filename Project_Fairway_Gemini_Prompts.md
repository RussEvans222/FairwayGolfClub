# Project Fairway — Gemini Image Prompts (v2 — Lighter)

## Design Direction Summary
**Bays:** Dark charcoal gray walls (NOT black) with visible architectural ceiling grid, multiple overhead light sources, warm white LED accents. Focused but not cave-like — more "modern recording studio" than "underground bunker." Clean walls with no visible equipment clutter. Auto-tee with sloped ball return.
**Social/Lounge:** Warm, airy, premium — cognac leather, natural wood, exposed brick, generous natural light from tall windows. Think "friend's luxury golf barn on a sunny afternoon."
**Context:** Historic arts campus (Workhouse Arts Center) — exposed brick, tall original windows with natural light flooding in, steel beams, industrial bones softened with warm modern finishes.
**Tech philosophy:** All technology is discreet and integrated — cameras are flush-mounted behind acoustic panels or recessed into walls, launch monitors are compact ceiling units, data displays are sleek wall-mounted screens. No visible wiring, no bulky rigs, no industrial arms. The tech works but doesn't shout.

## Usage Notes

- **Run one at a time** in Gemini for best results
- **If still too dark**, append: "Make the overall exposure 1-2 stops brighter. Add more visible overhead lighting fixtures. The walls should be medium charcoal gray, not dark."
- **If too bright / loses focus feel**, append: "Darken the bay walls slightly while keeping the overhead lighting bright."
- **For variations**: "Generate 3 variations with slightly different camera angles"
- **To adjust style**: Replace "Photorealistic" with "3D architectural rendering, V-Ray quality, bright studio lighting" for concept-art feel
- **For mood boards**: Ask Gemini for "4-panel mood board combining: [paste 2-3 prompts]"
- **Key phrase for Gemini if it goes too dark**: "This is NOT a nightclub or dark cave. It's a well-lit modern performance studio with dark WALLS but bright OVERHEAD lighting."
- **For the logo/branding shots**: If Gemini renders the text poorly, try: "The text 'FAIRWAY GOLF CLUB' must be spelled exactly and clearly readable in brushed gold metal lettering."
