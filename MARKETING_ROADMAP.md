## Positioning

**Local market gap, not a generic simulator pitch.** The core local argument: 5 traditional golf courses within 15 minutes of Lorton prove demand, but zero indoor simulator options exist there — golfers currently drive 30+ minutes to dated, non-premium alternatives (CAFDExGO, Uni Indoor Golf, GOLFTEC, Five Iron, ParCiti). Fairway fills a real, provable local gap rather than competing in an already-crowded urban simulator market.

**Target demographic:** Lorton median household income ~$138K, 89% white-collar workforce — a high-value local audience currently underserved.

**Differentiator:** Golfer360 — AI-powered performance profiles that persist across every visit. No competitor in the 15-minute radius offers this. Reinforced by the founder's own background as a working **Salesforce Application Developer** who is personally coding the entire tech backend in-house — technology risk is de-risked, not outsourced, and it's the reason CapEx can be capped at ~$70K instead of ~$150K (see `OPERATIONAL_PLAN.md`).

**Local roots story:** Russell Evans is a Crosspointe resident, living less than 1 mile from the target site. Lead with this in any local/community/government-facing conversation — it's a stronger and more specific story than "lives nearby."

## The Marketing Flywheel

The Golfer360 customer journey (full detail in `PRODUCT_REQUIREMENTS.md`) *is* the marketing flywheel — each phase feeds the next:

```
Phase 1: Mobile Unit at Workhouse community events
   → free 15-min swing check/putting games, QR-code scan-to-play
   → instant Golfer_Profile__c signups (warm-lead pipeline before the site even opens)
        ↓
Phase 2: Touchless kiosk check-in at the physical lounge
   → converts warm leads + walk-ins into paying sessions
   → idle wait time gets redirected to a Bunnyman drink (cross-promotion, not lost time)
        ↓
Phase 3: Connected bay experience
   → personalized welcome KPIs make every return visit feel earned/tracked
   → this is the retention engine — data-driven "come back and see your progress" hook
        ↓
Phase 4: Frictionless auto-tee
   → removes the last piece of manual annoyance, protects the premium feel that justifies price
        ↓
   back to Phase 1: satisfied members refer friends, corporate groups, and the community events keep refilling the top of the funnel
```

This reframes "Fairway Mobile" from a standalone revenue line (as it's described in the business plan) into the **explicit top of a documented flywheel** — every mobile event exists to feed Phase 2/3 retention, not just to sell one-off bookings.

## Local Strategic Partnership — Bunnyman Brewing

- **Partner:** Bunnyman Brewing Cafe at the Workhouse (on-campus location).
- **Strategic framing:** a highly collaborative neighbor strategy, not isolated commercial retail — actively building a relationship with a potential on-campus neighbor rather than just co-existing near one.
- **Collaborative concepts being explored:**
  - Bunnyman craft beer on tap for Fairway members.
  - Exclusive co-branded seasonal releases — e.g. a **"Fairway Golden Ale."**
  - Structured **"stay-and-play"** cross-promotional programs (play a round, walk over for a beer; both businesses benefit from shared foot traffic).
  - Kiosk-driven cross-promotion: Phase 2 of the Golfer360 journey routes walk-ins to Bunnyman while they wait for a bay to open (see Marketing Flywheel above).
