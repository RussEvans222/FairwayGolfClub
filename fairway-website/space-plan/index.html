Section 1

Space Program

This is a space requirement, not a fit-out of any specific building — the footprint below is sized only to what the program actually needs, so a location owner can judge whether their available space (whatever shape it happens to be) can reasonably accommodate it. Entrance/reception opens directly onto a large lounge on one side (no wall — open threshold) and a hallway on the other. That hallway leads immediately to two open-concept bays sitting right behind the lounge, so the social scene stays visible from the seating area, then continues back further to two enclosed private bays, both restrooms, and the mechanical room — each with its own independent door off the hallway.

Section 4

Infrastructure & Utility Requirements

Electrical

  • Recommended: 400A service, sized for all four bays plus the lounge and office load from day one
  • Per-bay draw: ~2,500–5,000W across 3–4 dedicated circuits (projector, gaming PC, launch monitor, auto-tee/ball return, lighting/AV)

HVAC / Climate Control

  • Each bay produces 5,000–10,000 BTU/hr of waste heat — plan for 1–2 additional tons of cooling per bay beyond base HVAC
  • Target consistent temperature: 68–72°F

Networking

  • Business-class fiber preferred, minimum 100 Mbps symmetric
  • Hardwired CAT6 to each bay; separate guest WiFi network for customer devices

Acoustics

  • Golf ball impacts peak at 85–95 dB — acoustic panels needed on shared walls and ceiling
  • Private bays: solid walls + door provide most of the isolation from the hallway; standard acoustic treatment applies
  • Open bays: only a low partition or ceiling-mounted net separates them from each other and the lounge, so they need heavier acoustic paneling and ceiling baffles to keep impact noise from carrying into the social space
  • Rubber flooring or thick turf over concrete in all bays to absorb impact vibration

Plumbing (New — Wash & Change Rooms)

  • Two wet walls (shower stalls), floor drains, and waterproof membrane
  • Water heater / tankless unit sized for two simultaneous showers at full four-bay occupancy
  • Dedicated exhaust fan per restroom (80–110 CFM) for moisture control
Section 5

Questions for Ownership / Landlord

Space & Structure

  • Is there a column grid or structural obstruction within the target footprint?
  • What is the existing clear ceiling height, and is 12' achievable?
  • What is the floor load capacity?

Utilities

  • What electrical service is currently available, and is there a path to 400A?
  • Are there existing sanitary/water lines near the proposed restroom location, or would new lines need to be run?
  • Is there drain capacity for two additional shower stalls?

Lease

  • What lease term flexibility is available?
  • Does the space allow for the private/open bay mix and staff office build-out described above, or are there constraints on interior partition changes?

Access

  • What loading/delivery access exists for simulator equipment?
  • What parking is available for members and staff?

Alignment

  • Are there use restrictions that would conflict with a small bar/lounge component?

Deal-Breakers / Non-Negotiables

  • Less than 10' clear ceiling height in the bay area
  • No reasonable path to ~4,134 sq ft for the full four-bay layout (shape can flex — the total area and adjacencies are what matter)
  • No reasonable path to 400A electrical service
  • No usable equipment delivery access (freight elevator or ground-level access)
  • Zoning or lease restrictions that prohibit a bar/lounge component
This page is a planning reference, not a finished proposal — all figures are subject to revision after a walkthrough and architect review.
